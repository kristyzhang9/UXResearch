Think-Aloud Protocol

In this research methodology, researchers provide users with a well-defined task. This task can be goal-oriented and straighforward or it can be exploratory. Users are asked to think-aloud through their actions and their decision-making during the task.

Pros (+)

researcher able to see mental model of performing the task, can gather information regarding user intent, can be done quickly

Cons (-)

hard for users to do it properly, changes how user may do task because they are being asked to think aloud and they are being watched

Tips

If the user stops thinking aloud, prompt them by saying "please think aloud as you're completing the task."

Provide an example of you completing an unrelated task and thinking aloud.




Source: User-centered Research and Evaluation course at Carnegie Mellon University
