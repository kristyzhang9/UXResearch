Contextual Inquiry

In a contextual inquiry, users are first asked a set of standard questions through a semi-structured interview. They are then observed and questioned while they work in their own environments. This allows researchers to be in the context of the problem space.

Pros (+)

see user's natural behavior, see context around a user that might inform decisions, can witness challenges and gain empathy for user

Cons (-)

time-consuming, not 100% natural, expensive

Tips

The user is the master. As you are observing the user in his or her natural environment, remember that you are there to learn from the user. Let the user lead the observation portion.

Ask questions throughout the observation portion and record as many details as you can. If you are recording over audio or video, make sure to ask for consent!
